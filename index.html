Responsive Redesign

Improving the usability of a local service website


Introduction

Goal: identify usability and accessibility issues in a public webpage and create a new design for it

How:

  • analyze the existing page and look for problems
  • design low-fi and high-fi prototypes for the new page
  • create the new responsive page in HTML/CSS compatible with 3 device sizes

The website of choice: https://www.bccls.org/

I chose this website for my local library account at home. I use this once every couple of months and every time, I find myself having to scan the full page of irregularly laid out irreleveant information to find the important stuff I want to access.

Identifying Problems

I analyzed the existing webpage, based on usability and accessibility criteria. These issues provided goals to target in the redesign process.

Usability

Biggest reasons why I chose this website: layout and lack of responsiveness

  • layout is cluttered and distracting
    • no specific section draws the eyes as being important (except for “apply for an ecard!” button which is styled completely differently to stand out as if it is the thing users want to look for)
    • as a user, most commonly used sections are the search, login, and find a library sections, which are not clearly distinguished among other random information such as pet pictures
    • no clear reading order or visual hierarchy to match the level of relevance of different information
    • partial grid layout but cells are misaligned and all uniform despite varying levels of importance
  • inconsistent text styling, especially among links makes unintuitive distinctions between seemingly similar types of text
    • headings match, but description text varies in size and weight
    • “click here” is different in the "Want Ads" section than in the "Did You Know…” section and from the links in the “Readers' Advisory”
  • much of page content corresponds to info found in dropdown menus but often with different names
    • “eBCCLS” section is under Digital Collections
    • “Did You Know…” section wants to highlight services which is under Services
    • “Be a Champion” is the same as Support Your Library
  • slideshow in center highlights current events and services which are repeated in other sections
  • other redundant or vaguely named content
    • “Limited Public Services Offered at Select Libraries!” is outdated announcement linking to post-COVID reopenings, though “public services” is misleading considering services has different meaning in navbar
    • What is “Browse Search” under catalog search input? just takes you to an empty page with a search bar
    • Why is “My Title Lists” under the login?
  • page is fixed width, with no responsiveness for screen size
  • mobile site is just a completely different webpage with different styles for many components
    • page first displays the desktop site on load which is too big detailed for the phone, mobile site loads after a few seconds
    • scrolling to the bottom of this site shows actual navigation menu with a button to show full desktop site
    • also a button to open “classic mobile site” which is a whole other site with its own problems that we don't have the time to get into…
  • most of the information from the desktop site is hidden
    • pros: distills down clutter and highlights essential functionality unlike desktop site
    • cons: poor memorability, disorienting when switching devices especially for users used to one or other

Accessibility

WebAIM WAVE was used to detect accessibility issues on the page. Here's a summary of what it reported:

  • page language not specified
  • many HTML form elements (text input, dropdowns, radio buttons, etc.) that do not have attached labels
    • personally, unsure of the severity of this given there is descriptive text next to them so even if not explicitly using a <label> attached to the inputs, screen readers should provide some description
  • some images have alternative text but some do not
  • only a couple of random ARIA tags, no consistent use of them
  • most of the page made good use of hierarchical elements like different heading tags, except for the navigation menu at the bottom
  • “forgot your password?” text is too small in login section
  • many flagged contrast errors for the white headings on light blue backgrounds
    • after desaturating the page most do not seem too severe except for one under the “Want Ads” section

I agreed with most of these errors though I was unsure about some of the contrast and form label issues. I also noticed some things that seemed like issues to me that weren't flagged by WAVE such as the fact that the buttons are images not text, though they do have alt text for screenreaders.
